1. Confirm English

Firstly, we need to agree on basic English sentence structors and grammar. This will allow us to make comparisons between English and German effectively

2. Basic German Sentences

Secondly, we need to compare a few simple English sentence against a German Sentence

3. Nomitive Case - 1st Party

The Nomitive Case is always at the beginning of an english sentence. "I went shopping" and "I am swimming" This is basic example of a nomitive case its about who or what is doing the action.

4. Accusative Case - 2nd Party

This is the part of an english sentence where something is being done to it. "I threw a ball" in this instance you threw a ball. You are the subject/Nomitive and the ball is the direct Object/Accusative.

5. Dative Case - 3rd Party

This part of the sentence involves a 3rd party. "I threw a ball against a wall" I am the subject/Nomitive the ball is the Direct Object/Accusative and the wall is the Indirect object/Dative

6. Genative Case - 3rd Party

This part allocates ownership of a particular item within the sentence.
